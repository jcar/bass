Reading Fish Behavior with Forward-Facing Sonar
Palaniuk relies on forward-facing sonar to assess fish and activity before ever making a cast. He looks at the distance between the head and tail on screen—greater separation often signals a better-quality fish. This visual detail allows Palaniuk to prioritize casts and stay efficient, targeting the right fish.
Spotted Bass Patterns and Location Strategy
Throughout the day, Palaniuk finds that these fish often group by size, varying from section to section. Recognizing these patterns lets him shift quickly between areas and be selective. Palaniuk also utilizes MEGA Live 2 to understand how different fish respond to pressure, retrieve speed, and bait movement.
Structure Scanning and Precise Presentation
Accurate scanning is key to unlocking the full potential of forward-facing sonar. By making small, deliberate movements with the trolling motor, Palaniuk gets multiple angles on key cover. With the improved clarity of MEGA Live 2, he picks apart submerged trees and identifies prime ambush spots. Small changes in viewing angles may reveal hidden limbs, isolated fish, or changes in bottom composition.
