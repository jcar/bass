TITLE: How to Punch Through Grass Like a Pro: Kevin VanDam’s Winning Technique 💥 · The Official Web Site of Kevin VanDam

Whether you’re targeting Florida hydrilla mats or clutching massive bass hiding in thick cover, Kevin VanDam—legendary angler and multiple Bassmaster Classic champion—shares his go-to gear and strategy for punching through grass like a pro. Here’s what the video teaches:
1. Gear Up Right 🛠️
-
Heavy Rod & High-Speed Reel
VanDam uses a 7’10” Lew’s punching rod paired with a Lew’s HyperMag reel (8.3:1) to deliver a punch setup that’s fast for making follow-up casts—not necessarily for landing fish
-
Lead Tungsten Weight + Braid
A 1.5 oz Strike King Tour Grade tungsten weight pegged with a bobber stop, on 65-lb braid, gives maximum punching power and zero stretch -
Hook and Bait Combo
The classic pairing: 4/0 Mustad Denny Brauer Grip Pin hook and a Strike King Punch Bug. Deep ribs and soft profile help the hook stay hidden while avoiding snags
2. Read the Mats & Fish Smart 🧭
-
Pick the Greenest Mats
Look for lush, vibrant green mats—they’re the most fertile, untreated, and likely to hold bass. Avoid stale, brown mats as they lack cover and forage

Don’t Whisper—Make Noise
The impact of your weight punching through the mat often attracts fish. Mats are naturally noisy; don’t worry about being stealthy here
– related link: kevinvandam.com.
3. Execute the Punch with Precision 🎯
-
Launch High: Cast hard and high to give the weight momentum needed to penetrate the dense cover
-
Feel for the Bite: Watch for subtle mat movements—often that’s a fish grabbing your bait under the grass
-
Let It Fall: Once through, keep your rod high (about 10 o’clock) and maintain slack so the bait falls freely into the strike zone
-
Three-Four Shakes: Tend to give 3–4 shakes under the mat, sometimes lifting the bait just under the surface to tempt a bite
4. Timing Is Everything ⏰
-
Cold Fronts & Heat Waves:
After cooler fronts, bass bury deeper under mats, and in summer heat, they use mats as shade. Either way—punching becomes deadly effective -
Check the Bite Style:
Aggressive fish hit on the drop. If bites are sluggish, pause and jig your bait a bit longer to grab attention .
5. Stay Patient & Persistent 🎣
This technique is known for producing trophy bass—but also smaller catches. VanDam stresses that consistency and endurance are key. You won’t always land giants, but persistence will lead to big rewards
Why This Works (Backed by KVD Expertise)
-
Max Penetration with Weight: Tungsten’s density pushes through dense mats better than traditional lead.
-
High-Speed Reel = Efficiency: More casts per hour means more opportunities.
-
Natural Lure Profile: The Punch Bug’s soft, ribbed design camouflages the hook while swaying in grass.
-
Controlled Presentation: High rod angle and slack line make bites more noticeable.
-
Environmental Awareness: Timing with weather and vegetation health helps target prime fish zones.
Final Takeaway
If you fish heavy grass or mats—especially during Florida summers or cold snaps—VanDam’s method equips you with everything you need:
-
Right gear: heavy rod, high-speed reel, tungsten weight, braid, and strong hook + bait.
-
Accurate targeting: pick the liveliest mats.
-
Intentional presentation: execute high-power casts, watch for nudges, and impart subtle action.
-
Environmental timing: use it during temperature transitions and peak vegetation.
-
Endurance: punch enough, and the big bass will show.
