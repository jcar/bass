Fishing with Jigs
Versatility of Jigs
- When I’m fishing the heaviest cover, focusing on a vertical presentation, I prefer using a Hack Attack jig.
- There’s no reason I can’t take this jig and, while casting along and reeling in, spot a target to pitch to.
- I can pitch it into a bush, hole in the grass, or any heavy cover, and fish it like a flipping jig.
Hack Attack Jig for Vertical Presentations
- When focused on vertical presentations, I favor the Hack Attack jig, especially for heavy cover.
- I always opt for a heavier jig rather than a lighter one.
Fast Fall Advantage
- A fast fall mimics a bowling ball coming through the water, prompting a quick reaction from fish.
- They don’t have time to think and often strike out of impulse.
- For flipping, my go-to size is an ¾ ounce Hack Attack jig.
Choosing Trailers for Jigs
- I typically use a Rage Craw trailer on my jig.
- For a more compact option, a Scon Bug or even a Menace can be used.
- A smaller trailer will increase the fall rate, making the jig drop faster.
Size Variations for Different Cover
- Jigs come in various sizes, from as light as 3/8 ounce to over an ounce.
- Heavier jigs can punch through extremely heavy cover effectively.
Punching through Thick Mats
- When I’m punching through the thickest and gnarliest mats, I prefer using a big weight with a single hook and a punch bug.
- This setup penetrates heavy cover better than a flipping jig.
Flipping Jigs as a Trigger
- A flipping jig remains an excellent tool to trigger fish to bite.
- The speed of the fall is crucial in eliciting a reaction from fish.
